Clone a voice. Make it speak.

Parrot clones a voice from a few seconds of audio and speaks any text you type — entirely on your own machine. No accounts, no API keys, no cloud. Your reference audio never leaves your device.

Download for Windows View on GitHub

Windows 10 & 11 (64-bit) · CUDA or CPU, auto-detected

Two steps, that's it

Parrot does exactly two things, and tries to do them without making you fight it.

Step 1

Clone a voice

Record a few seconds with your mic or drop in an audio clip. Parrot saves it as a reusable voice profile — no training run, no waiting.

Step 2

Make it speak

Type any text, pick a saved voice, and get natural speech back. Play it in the app or export a .wav.

~600 languages

Zero-shot multilingual synthesis with automatic language detection — or pick a language yourself.

Reusable profiles

Cloned voices are saved locally and ready to reuse. Lock one to get the same result every single time.

Runs on your GPU

NVIDIA CUDA when you have it, CPU when you don't — auto-detected. No queue, no rate limit.

Private

Your reference audio and generated speech never leave your machine. No upload, no server, no tracking.

Yours forever

One model download, then fully offline. No account, no API key, no subscription — ever.

Open source

Apache-2.0 licensed and built in the open. Read the code, build it yourself, make it yours.
